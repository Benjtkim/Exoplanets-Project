Welcome to the Habitable Exoplanet Simulator!

An exoplanet is defined as any planet that orbits a star outside of our solar system. So far, NASA has found hundreds of exoplanets. This simulation depicts just those that could potentially support life as we know it. The bar towards the bottom of the screen represents distance in terms of light years. A planet at the very front of the bar where it is 0 would be 0 light years away from the Earth. This means the further you scroll, the further the planets that pop up are from the Earth. A light year is the distance that light travels in a year. Given that light travels 299,792,458 meters in a second, a light year is a mind-bogglingly vast distance. Even if we could travel at the speed of light, it would still take us 4 years to get to the nearest potentially habitable exoplanet, Proxima Centuari B. If we wanted to travel to the furthest potentially habitable exoplanet, it would take us 4,976 years! Hover over an exoplanet to see its statistics. They're self-explanatory.
